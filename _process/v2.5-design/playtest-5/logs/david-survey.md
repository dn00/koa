## Part 1: Open-Ended First (answer before reading the rest of the survey)

**O1:** Describe your experience in 2-3 sentences. What stood out?

The game rewards systematic analysis. Each puzzle is a constraint-satisfaction problem: maximize damage while minimizing scrutiny, with tag compatibility acting as the primary constraint. What stood out most was the corroboration mechanic --- discovering that same-tag pairs yield bonus damage was satisfying, and it immediately became my primary strategy.

**O2:** What was the single best moment across all 3 puzzles?

Getting FLAWLESS on Puzzle 2. I had internalized the refutation + corroboration opening from Puzzle 1, identified the trap card (phone_gps with AWAY tag), and executed cleanly. It felt like applying a debugging methodology to a new codebase --- same patterns, different data.

**O3:** What was the single worst moment across all 3 puzzles?

Puzzle 3, Attempt 1: playing smartwatch (ACTIVE) + doorbell_cam (IDLE) and getting hit with conflict(1). I did not know ACTIVE and IDLE were opposing tags. The game never told me which tags oppose each other. That felt like a hidden rule rather than a discoverable mechanic.

**O4:** If you could change one thing about this game, what would it be?

Show the opposition axes somewhere. Even a subtle hint like color-coding tags or grouping them in pairs (ASLEEP/AWAKE, HOME/AWAY, ACTIVE/IDLE, ALONE/ACCOMPANIED) would let players reason about contradictions without memorizing an undocumented lookup table.

**M12:** Is there anything broken, unfair, or confusing we haven't asked about? (open-ended)

1. Tag opposition pairs are not documented anywhere in the briefing. Players must discover ASLEEP/AWAKE, HOME/AWAY, ACTIVE/IDLE, and ALONE/ACCOMPANIED through trial and error. The first two pairs are intuitive; ACTIVE/IDLE is not.

2. The double-contradiction block on same-tag pairs is a subtle edge case. If you play two IDLE cards after committing ACTIVE, the first card triggers the warning and the second card causes a full block. The player loses their turn attempt with no way to predict this from the UI. Consider: should the warning apply to the whole pair, not per-card?

3. Puzzle 3 appears to have a THOROUGH ceiling --- I could not find any card ordering that achieves scrutiny <= 2 with all concerns addressed. If this is intentional, it might frustrate completionists. If it is unintentional, it is a balance bug.

4. The "proves" field on cards like fitbit (ALERTNESS) and security_cam (LIVENESS) don't match any concern in Puzzle 1. This means those cards contribute damage but not progress on concerns. This could confuse players who expect every "proves" to matter. Consider labeling concern-relevant proofs differently from non-concern proofs.

5. Minor: when a turn is blocked by contradiction, the game says "Turn not consumed --- try different cards" but in a non-interactive pipe context this effectively ends the game. Not a real-world issue if the game is interactive, but worth noting for automated testing.
