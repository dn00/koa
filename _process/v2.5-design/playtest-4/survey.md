## Part 4: Scaled Assessment

Rate 1-7. **1 = strongly disagree, 7 = strongly agree.**

### Engagement
**S1:** I wanted to keep playing after the session ended.
**S2:** I found myself thinking about strategies between puzzles.
**S3:** The 3-puzzle session felt like the right amount. *(1 = too short/long, 7 = perfect)*

### Learning & Progression
**S4:** The second puzzle felt different from the first (not just a reskin).
**S5:** Skills I learned in Puzzle 1 helped me in later puzzles.
**S6:** I felt like I was getting better as I played more puzzles.
**S7:** The difficulty progression felt natural.

### Clarity
**S8:** When I lost, I understood why.
**S9:** The feedback after each turn was useful.
**S10:** I was confused about what I was supposed to do. *(reverse-scored)*

### Frustration
**S11:** I felt frustrated by things outside my control. *(reverse-scored)*
**S12:** When I made a mistake, it felt like MY mistake.
**S13:** The game was too hard. *(reverse-scored)*
**S14:** The game was too easy. *(reverse-scored)*

### Achievement
**S15:** I cared about getting a better badge.
**S16:** Winning felt earned.
**S17:** The scrutiny breakdown after the game helped me understand what happened. *(new — tests post-game breakdown)*
